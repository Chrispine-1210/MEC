# Structured Slide Notes

## Slide 1: Cover

Open with the full ecosystem positioning: Mtendere Education has moved beyond a website into a governed digital platform and administration system.

## Slide 2: Table of contents

Use this as the executive route map; the deck moves from strategic value to architecture, then public experience, admin operations, controls, gaps, and roadmap.

## Slide 3: Executive summary

Emphasize the dual value: student-facing conversion and admin-side operating control.

## Slide 4: Company overview

Frame the ecosystem around institutional modernization, student success, partnerships, and scalable operations.

## Slide 5: Ecosystem at a glance

Show how public journeys, admin controls, APIs, data, security, and integrations operate as one system.

## Slide 6: Architecture overview

Explain the technical stack in business language while keeping concrete labels from the codebase.

## Slide 7: Data and operating domains

Use the 47-table schema as proof that this is an operational platform, not a static site.

## Slide 8: End-to-end journey

Walk from discovery to application to admin decision and reporting.

## Slide 9: Client platform

Point to homepage, navigation, search, hero, services, testimonials, and CTA value.

## Slide 10: Scholarships and jobs

Position opportunities as conversion engines with filters, detail pages, applications, and uploads.

## Slide 11: Events, partners, content

Show engagement and credibility modules that support community, partner, and SEO growth.

## Slide 12: Student workspace

Explain login, dashboard, application tracker, referrals, checkout, and mobile readiness.

## Slide 13: Admin operating model

Present the admin dashboard as the management command surface.

## Slide 14: Ecosystem command center

Highlight module health, automation readiness, and cross-system visibility.

## Slide 15: Content governance

Show repeatable CRUD, lifecycle, media, export, duplicate, and analytics patterns.

## Slide 16: Events and partners

Explain why these are enterprise modules: registrations, CRM, documents, agreements, activities, finance, and renewals.

## Slide 17: Application pipeline

Show administrative efficiency through structured review, scoring, shortlist, interview, and approve/reject decisions.

## Slide 18: Users, roles, settings

Connect RBAC and settings to secure institutional control.

## Slide 19: Analytics and automation

Show the reporting loop from user activity to leadership insight and scheduled-report readiness.

## Slide 20: Security

Explain layered defenses: identity, authorization, API protection, admin governance, data operations.

## Slide 21: UI/UX review

Balance strengths with professional improvement opportunities.

## Slide 22: Performance and scalability

Position the platform as cloud-ready while naming specific hardening work.

## Slide 23: Recommendations

Use the priority matrix to guide leadership action.

## Slide 24: Roadmap

Lay out hardening, operations intelligence, AI-assisted growth, and regional scale.

## Slide 25: Closing

End with institutional impact and the modernization thesis.
